# Scheduling: Volunteer Guide

This guide is for volunteers and shadows. It covers how to see your shifts, keep
your availability current, and request a swap or drop when you cannot make a
clinic.

Directors building schedules should read the [Director Guide](./scheduling-directors.md).
For how the system works under the hood, see the [technical reference](./scheduling.md).

## Finding your schedule

Open **Schedule** in the navigation and you land on **My schedule**
(`/schedule`). This page shows your upcoming shifts for the active term, your
current availability, and any swap or drop requests you have open.

If you want to see the whole clinic for a given Saturday, not just your own
shifts, open **Full schedule** (`/schedule/full`) and pick a date. It lists every
department's directors, volunteers, and shadows for that day.

## Reading a shift

Each shift tells you four things:

- **Date.** The clinic Saturday. All dates display in clinic time, so a Saturday
  is the same day no matter where you are.
- **Department.** The team you are working with that day.
- **Role.** One of:
  - **Volunteer**: a standard clinic shift.
  - **Director**: you are running the department's clinic that day.
  - **Shadow**: you are observing.
- **Tags** (volunteers only). Any of triage, walk-in, CC (continuity care), or
  remote, set by your director to show your role for the day.

You can hold shifts in more than one department, and your role can differ by
department on the same day.

## Keeping your availability current

Your availability is the list of clinic dates you can work. Directors build the
schedule from it, so keeping it accurate is the single most helpful thing you can
do.

On **My schedule**, update your availability by selecting the clinic dates you
can attend and saving. Your selection takes effect immediately and replaces
whatever availability was previously assumed for you.

A few things to know:

- Your saved dates take priority over the baseline availability captured from
  your application.
- A director can override your availability if they need to (for example, after
  talking with you directly). If your director has set an override, that is what
  the schedule uses.
- If you see an **acknowledge** prompt for your availability, confirming it lets
  your director know you have reviewed what applies to you.

## Requesting a swap

A swap trades your shift with another volunteer's shift on a different date. Use
this when you cannot make your date but a teammate can take it and you can take
theirs.

From **My schedule**, choose the shift you want to swap and pick an eligible
partner and their date. A partner is eligible only when:

- They are assigned to the date you name, and
- They hold the **same role** as you (volunteer for volunteer, director for
  director), and
- They are not a shadow.

You cannot swap a volunteer shift for a director shift, and you cannot swap into
or out of a shadow slot.

## Dropping a shift

A drop removes you from a shift without naming a replacement. Use this when you
simply cannot make a clinic.

From **My schedule**, choose the shift and submit a drop. You can add a short
note explaining why.

Shadow shifts can **only** be dropped, never swapped, because a shadow is an
observer slot rather than a tradeable position.

## After you submit a request

- Your request starts as **pending**.
- A director (or a schedule manager) reviews it and either **approves** or
  **denies** it.
- When a swap is approved, the assignments update automatically: you move to your
  partner's date and they move to yours, keeping the same role.
- You can **cancel** your own request any time while it is still pending.
- You cannot have two pending requests open for the same shift at once.

You will see the current status of each request on **My schedule**.

## HIPAA compliance and your shifts

Working clinic requires current HIPAA training. Directors see a compliance flag
next to scheduled volunteers, so if your certification is expired or missing, you
may be asked to renew before clinic. Keep your certificate current from **My
Info** to avoid any last-minute issues.

## Quick answers

**I can't make my shift and nobody can take it.** Submit a drop with a note.

**I found someone to switch with.** Submit a swap naming them and their date.
They must hold the same role as you.

**Can I swap my shadow shift?** No. Shadow shifts can only be dropped.

**I changed my mind after requesting.** Cancel the request while it is still
pending.

**My availability changed for the rest of the term.** Update your availability on
My schedule rather than dropping shifts one at a time.

**Why is there a warning on my name?** Most often your HIPAA certificate needs
renewing. Check My Info.
