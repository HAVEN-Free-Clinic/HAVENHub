# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
HAVEN Hub serves the HAVEN Free Clinic at Yale University. Four audiences sign in here:
- **Volunteer clinicians and students** — Yale medical/PA students and volunteer providers signing in before or between clinic shifts. Often hurried, frequently on mobile.
- **Clinic directors and admins** — leadership and coordinators running scheduling, recruitment, and compliance, mostly on desktop.
- **New recruits onboarding** — first-time invited users who may not yet know what HAVEN Hub is. The login is their first impression of the product.
- **IT and operational staff** — sign in routinely; value speed.

The job to be done at this screen is singular and simple: authenticate (almost always via "Sign in with Yale" / Microsoft Entra ID) and get into the app.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy and institutional. Calm, credible, clearly affiliated with Yale and HAVEN — it should read as official healthcare infrastructure, not a startup. Voice is plain and reassuring, never jargon-heavy or cute. Three words: **credible, calm, official**.

Naming: the product is "HAVEN Hub" (two words) in prose and UI; identifiers stay `havenhub`. No em-dashes in copy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined institutional minimalism. Anchored by Yale Blue (`#00356b`) with supporting brand tints already defined as design tokens. Inter as the type family. The left brand panel carries identity (logo + mission copy) over a tinted photograph of the Yale Physicians Building (800 Howard Avenue); the right panel stays clean and form-focused. The photo should sit under a Yale-blue tint/gradient so the logo and copy stay fully legible — branded overlay, not full-bleed drama. Anti-references: generic SaaS gradient-purple auth screens, glassmorphism, dark-mode-with-glow, anything that looks AI-templated.

### Design Principles
1. **Institutional trust first.** Every choice should reinforce that this is official Yale/HAVEN infrastructure. Legibility and credibility beat flourish.
2. **The form is the hero on the right; identity is the hero on the left.** Don't let the background image or motif compete with either.
3. **Fast for repeat users, clear for first-timers.** The primary "Sign in with Yale" action must be unmistakable in under 2 seconds; first-timers should never wonder where they are.
4. **Calm, plain language.** Reassuring microcopy, helpful (never blaming) errors, no jargon. No em-dashes.
5. **Restraint over decoration.** Yale Blue + tinted neutrals, generous whitespace, one focal moment. If a decorative element doesn't reinforce the brand, remove it.
